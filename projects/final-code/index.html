Masters of the Masters

Background of the Masters

The Masters is an annual major golf event. The Masters is considered to be one of the most famous tournaments in all of golf's history. Unlike other major championships every year it is held at the beautiful Augusta National Golf Club in Augusta, Georgia. It is one of four major championships in golf. It is the first of the four to occur and usually takes place in the first week of April. The tournament in total is four days long going from Thursday-Sunday. After Friday the field is cut down. So making it to the weekend at the Masters is a challenge in itself. The Masters were first founded by Bobby Jones and Clifford Roberts. The first ever Masters was played 87 years ago on March 22, 1934. The field of players for the Masters tournament is smaller than all the other major championships because this tournament is invitational only. When asked about what he thinks of the Masters, Univeristy of Maryland's Club Golf Vice President, Alex Pak said, "Magnolia Lane, the drive when you first enter the ground for Augusta national, there is just so much history everywhere you go." The tournament is an official money event of the PGA Tour, the European Tour, and the Japan Golf Tour. The prize money for winning the Masters is of course the famous green jacket, a trophy, your name forever engraved in the big trophy in the main clubhouse, and of course the $11.5 million.

The drive is the longest shot the golfer will hit. The average PGA Tour golfer hits their driver about 290 yards, which to put to scale is practically three football fields lined up. It brings a whole different side to a lot of these sports and really makes the sports more interesting. One Putt Percentage

On the complete opposite side of the spectrum you have putting. Putting in golf is performed with the club called the putter. When asked about putting Pak said, "I would say it's the most difficult part especially at the Masters and Augusta. At every major tournament the pros get a green reading book and Augusta doesn’t have those. So reading putts is more difficult out at Augusta for the pros unless you have experience on the course." There are a lot of factors that go into putting and it is definitely the hardest stroke to master in the game of golf. The putter is probably the club you will hit the softest. Some of these factors are the softness or hardness of the greens, the wind, temperature, condition of the greens, slope on the greens, and speed of the greens. One putting for PGA Tour golfers is still a challenge even though they are professionals. A one putt is when you get your ball onto the green and then in one stroke (putt) you hit your ball into the hole.

Patrick Reed: 45.99% (298 one putts on 648 holes)

Reed is a professional golfer from the United States. His world PGA Tour ranking is seventh. He is known for his very impressive short game. Reed won the Masters in 2018 and has nine total professional wins under his belt. Reed tied for eighth in this past year's Masters.

Scoring Average

Unlike a lot of other sports where the higher your score is the better, in golf it's actually the other way around. The lower your score in golf the better your score is. Scoring in golf can be very confusing for people that do not watch golf. Let's break it down. Each golf course has a par, the par of a course is the amount of strokes it would take an expert golfer to complete the course. Players can either be on par, under par, or over par. Your score is based off of that course's specific par. The course par usually ranges from 68-72 strokes. Your typical championship golf course will usually have a par of 72 that includes, four par threes, ten par fours, and four par fives. If the course par is a 72 and you shoot a 70 then your final score would be -2, which is considered two under. Each hole at a golf course has its own par too; they range from par 3 to par 5. If you are two under par its called an Eagle, if you are one under par it's a birdie, if you are on par and it's a par four hole that means you took four strokes to finish the hole, if you go one stroke over the par than its a bogey, two strokes over bar is double bogey.
